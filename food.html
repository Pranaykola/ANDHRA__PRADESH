GUTTI VANKAYA

Gutti Vankaya Curry, also known as Gutti Vankaya Kura (వంకాయ కూర) in Telugu, is a quintessential and highly celebrated dish from the rich culinary heritage of Andhra Pradesh. It's a delectable and flavorful curry made with small, tender eggplants that are meticulously stuffed with a spiced peanut and coconut masala, and then simmered in a tangy tamarind-based gravy.The preparation of Gutti Vankaya can be a bit time-consuming due to the stuffing process, but the resulting rich, flavorful, and deeply satisfying curry is well worth the effort, making it a beloved dish across Andhra households.



CHEPALA PULUSU

Chepala Pulusu (చేపల పులుసు), meaning "fish tamarind gravy," is a highly iconic and beloved dish in Andhra Pradesh and among Telugu-speaking communities worldwide. It's renowned for its distinctive tangy, spicy, and often robust flavor profile, making it a staple in coastal regions and a must-try for anyone exploring Andhra cuisine.In Ongole, being part of the coastal Andhra region, Chepala Pulusu is widely available and deeply cherished, with many local eateries specializing in this traditional preparation, particularly the famous "Nellore Chepala Pulusu" style.



PULIHORA

Pulihora (పులిహోర) is an extremely popular and deeply cherished rice dish in Andhra Pradesh, synonymous with festivals, celebrations, and temple offerings (prasadam). The name "Pulihora" literally translates to "sour rice," with "puli" meaning tangy or sour in Telugu, perfectly describing its characteristic flavor derived primarily from tamarind or lemon.In Ongole, being a part of Andhra Pradesh, Pulihora is a culinary staple, found everywhere from homes to restaurants, roadside stalls, and most importantly, as a sacred offering at temples. Its versatility and satisfying flavors ensure its enduring popularity.
