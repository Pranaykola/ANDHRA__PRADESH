PONGAL

In Andhra Pradesh, Pongal is celebrated with immense fervor and is popularly known as Makar Sankranti or Pedda Panduga . It is a major four-day harvest festival that marks the transition of the Sun into the Capricorn zodiac sign and the end of the winter solstice, heralding the beginning of longer days and the auspicious month of Magha. While the core essence of the festival giving thanks for a bountiful harvest to the Sun God, Mother Nature, and cattle remains the same as in Tamil Nadu, there are some unique traditions and names for each day in Andhra Pradesh.



UGADI

Ugadi, also known as Yugadi, is the most significant festival in Andhra Pradesh, marking the New Year according to the Hindu lunisolar calendar. It typically falls in March or April, on the first day of the Chaitra month, and symbolizes a fresh start, renewal, and the arrival of spring. The name "Ugadi" itself is derived from "Yuga" and "Adi", signifying the dawn of a new age. Ugadi in Andhra Pradesh is a beautiful celebration that blends rich traditions, delicious food, and deep philosophical meaning, marking the start of a new year with optimism, gratitude, and a mindful acceptance of life's varied flavors.



DUSSHERA

Dussehra, known as Vijayadashami or Dasara in Andhra Pradesh, is a highly significant and enthusiastically celebrated Hindu festival marking the triumph of good over evil. It culminates a nine-day period of worship dedicated to Goddess Durga and is observed with various distinct traditions across the state.Dussehra in Andhra Pradesh is a vibrant and deeply spiritual festival, characterized by elaborate decorations, devotional worship of Goddess Durga, unique cultural displays like Bommala Koluvu, and a strong emphasis on community and new beginnings.
